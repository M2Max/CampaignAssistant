Campaign ID: CAMP_006_NONPROFIT_AWARENESS
Campaign Name: "Stories That Matter" Awareness Campaign
Client: Global Water Initiative (Non-profit)
Industry: Non-profit / Environmental
Client Background: Global Water Initiative is an international non-profit focused on providing clean water access to rural communities in East Africa. They needed to increase donor awareness, engagement, and annual giving revenue by 40%.

Campaign Overview:
A documentary-style storytelling campaign across multiple channels highlighting the impact of their work. Used beneficiary stories, volunteer testimonials, and before/after community transformations to drive emotional connection and donations.

Marketing Approach:
- Documentary-style long-form video (15-minute YouTube film)
- 12-episode mini-series (2-3 minutes each) featuring individual beneficiary stories
- Podcast series: "Voices of Change" with founder interviews and field staff stories
- Social media content calendar: 6 posts daily across Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn
- Email campaign: Monthly impact newsletters and giving campaign emails
- Major donor outreach: Personal video messages from field teams
- Event marketing: 4 fundraising galas in major cities (NYC, LA, Chicago, Boston)
- PR strategy: Pitching stories to major publications

Budget Details:
- Total Campaign Budget: $89,000 USD
- Video Production (documentary + series): $35,000
- Podcast Production & Distribution: $8,000
- Social Media Management (6 months): $12,000
- Email Marketing Platform & Design: $6,000
- Event Production (4 galas): $18,000
- PR Agency Contract: $7,000
- Advertising/Paid Promotion: $3,000

Campaign Duration: 180 days (6 months, 2024)

Key Learnings:
- Beneficiary video testimonials generated 3x more donations than statistics/data
- Recurring donor program grew 18% faster after "Stories That Matter" campaign launch
- Email + social combo was 2.1x more effective for donations than either channel alone
- Event attendees who watched documentary beforehand donated 42% more
- Podcast attracted different donor demographic (higher income, professional background)

Campaign Outcome: Exceeded 40% growth target, achieving 51% increase in annual giving. Global Water Initiative established strong base of recurring donors and positioned themselves as the leading storyteller in water access non-profit space. Campaign became case study for non-profit digital marketing and won 2 industry awards.
