Campaign ID: CAMP_009_AUTOMOTIVE_REBRAND
Campaign Name: "DriveNext Genesis" Brand Relaunch Campaign
Client: DriveNext Motors
Industry: Automotive / EV
Client Background: DriveNext Motors, a 25-year-old car rental company, announced a pivot to 100% electric vehicles and needed to rebrand from "boring rental company" to "innovative EV mobility leader" targeting environmentally conscious business travelers and leisure renters.

Campaign Overview:
A comprehensive brand relaunch campaign repositioning DriveNext as a sustainability leader and tech-forward company. Used storytelling around environmental impact, innovation, and customer experience transformation to rebuild brand perception.

Marketing Approach:
- Documentary film: 10-minute brand story documentary showing transformation
- Social media: Behind-the-scenes content, sustainability messaging, customer testimonials
- Out-of-home advertising: Billboard campaign in major cities highlighting EV benefits
- Press strategy: Major business and auto publications, sustainability focused
- Influencer partnerships: 12 sustainability influencers and eco-conscious creators
- Event marketing: 8 launch events across major markets (NYC, LA, Chicago, Houston, etc.)
- Corporate partnerships: B2B partnerships with ESG-focused corporations
- Employee advocacy: Internal comms + employee social media ambassadors
- Digital video: YouTube pre-rolls, streaming platform ads
- Direct mail: Luxury packaging to top 10K previous customers

Budget Details:
- Total Campaign Budget: $520,000 USD
- Video Production (documentary + ads): $130,000
- Out-of-Home Advertising: $120,000
- Media Buy (Digital): $100,000
- Launch Events (8 cities): $90,000
- Influencer Partnerships: $45,000
- PR & Media Relations: $20,000
- Direct Mail: $15,000

Campaign Duration: 180 days (3-month intensive + 3-month sustained)

Results & Performance Metrics:

- Brand awareness in target market: +47% (measured via survey)
- Brand perception shift (positive sentiment): +64%
- Net Promoter Score (NPS): 72 (up from 41 pre-campaign)
- Brand consideration increase: +38%
- Environmental positioning: 76% now associate DriveNext with sustainability

Documentary & Video Performance:
- Documentary views: 287,438
- Documentary average watch time: 8.3 minutes (of 10)
- YouTube ads impressions: 18,247,293

- Video engagement rate: 3.2%

- User-generated content: 8,247 pieces of customer content
- Social sentiment (positive): 84%

Out-of-Home Performance:

- Returning customer bookings: 18,909 (40% of total)

- Price sensitivity: Minimal; EV focus drove willingness to book
- Average booking length: 3.2 days
- Average booking value: $687

Market Impact:
- Market share growth: +8.4% in target markets (vs. pre-campaign)
- Competitor awareness of EV trend: 89% of major competitors announced EV plans within 6 months
- Industry positioning: DriveNext recognized as EV leader in automotive rental
- Investor interest: Stock price +23% within campaign period

Key Learnings:
- Brand relaunch required consistency across all channels (integrated 8+ touchpoints)
- Sustainability messaging attracted different demographic (higher income, younger)
- Corporate B2B partnerships became larger revenue driver than consumer bookings
- Employee advocacy amplified reach: 34% of social engagement came from employee shares
- Documentary storytelling was most effective format for perception change
- Event marketing generated highest NPS (78) and loyalty indicators
- Video drove 47% of website traffic despite representing 23% of media spend

Long-Term Impact:
- Brand refresh maintained momentum through quarter 2
- Customer retention improved from 31% to 49% year-over-year
- Corporate program grew to 45% of total business by end of year

Campaign Outcome: Exceeded relaunch objectives by 34%. Transformed public perception of DriveNext from "traditional rental company" to "EV mobility innovator." Successfully shifted business model to corporate/B2B focus while growing consumer base. Positioned company for acquisition; acquired by major mobility company at 3.2x higher valuation than pre-campaign.
